01. Core Thesis

Mike is the constraint. Everything flows through him.

Every dollar of growth at Clickable Impact runs through one brain: Mike's. The "Idea to Revenue" methodology — positioning, offer stacks, landing pages, email sequences, advertorials, creative strategy, CRO — all of it lives in his head. That's a $4.3M business running on a single point of failure.

Caroline's genius zone is partnerships, investor outreach, and M&A. But she's stuck in ops meetings. Every hour Caroline spends on internal management is an hour not spent acquiring AmateurGolf.com or closing the next brand deal.

The play isn't "give them AI tools." The play is extract Mike's brain into a system, free Caroline from ops, and build the infrastructure that turns their $2M ad spend into actual margin.

"I am the bottleneck of the businesses."
— Mike Sierra, Onboarding Call 003
02. Bottleneck Matrix

10 Constraints, Ranked by Revenue Impact

Ordered by what's costing the most money right now. Tags show where the fix lives: human judgment, AI intelligence, or pure code/automation.

1
Mike = Only CRO/DR Marketing Brain
Everything from positioning to offer stacks to landing pages to email sequences goes through Mike. One person doing the work of a 6-person creative team. New products sit for weeks/months waiting for his bandwidth. The SwitchGrips launch sat on a garbage website because Mike hadn't gotten to it yet.
AI: Draft copy, pages, sequences Human: Mike approves & refines Code: Template system, auto-deploy

No Creative Performance Tracking
$2M/year in Meta ads with no system tracking which creative works at which funnel stage, for which persona, with which angle. Decisions are gut feel. ClickUp holds some of it, but it's not connected to actual performance data from Triple Whale or Meta.
AI: Analyze & categorize performance Code: Data pipeline from Triple Whale Human: Strategic creative decisions
3
Sales Team Ignoring CRM
Paid 6 figures to salespeople who don't log activities, don't follow up on time, and don't make their calls. Hot leads go cold. No visibility into pipeline health. No accountability system beyond Mike checking manually.
AI: Flag hot leads, daily task lists Code: CRM automation, alerts Human: Sales calls & relationship building
4
Caroline Trapped in Ops
Caroline raised $20M for 69 companies. Her genius zone is partnerships, investor outreach, and M&A. But she's being pulled into internal ops meetings, team management, and processes that should be systematized. Every hour in ops = one less hour pursuing the AmateurGolf.com acquisition or closing brand partnerships.
AI: Lead research, outreach drafts Code: Ops workflows, automations Human: Caroline closes deals
5
Idea-to-Revenue Pipeline Takes Months
Getting a new product from "in hand" to "generating revenue" requires: marketing angles, landing page, email sequences, infographics, product images, scripts, content creation, editing, ad launch, data analysis, iteration. Each step waits for the previous one. Most steps wait for Mike.
AI: Generate first drafts of everything Code: Automated deployment pipeline Human: Mike's strategic eye, final approval
6
1% Net Margin on $4.3M
~$46K net profit. $800K in seller-financing debt. $2M in ad spend eating margins. At this margin, a 5% efficiency gain on ad spend alone = $100K straight to the bottom line. This isn't a "nice to have" optimization — it's survival math.
AI: Ad spend optimization, CRO Code: Automated bid rules, reporting Human: Strategic budget allocation
7
100% Meta-Dependent for Paid Traffic
All $2M in ad spend goes to one platform. A single algorithm change or account suspension = catastrophic revenue drop. Mike wants YouTube, AppLovin, Snapchat, Pinterest, X, Reddit, and Connected TV but doesn't have bandwidth to test new channels.
AI: Creative adaptation per platform Code: Cross-platform deployment Human: Channel strategy decisions
8
Lead Gen is Manual
Caroline's outbound for newsletter sponsors, golf brands, and partnership opportunities is all manual research. Apollo and StoreLeads sit underutilized. Trade show follow-up is inconsistent. The pipeline for new business development is hand-cranked.
AI: Prospect research, scoring, outreach Code: Apollo/StoreLeads automation Human: Caroline qualifies & closes
9
Content Distribution is Ad Hoc
Content gets created but distribution across platforms is inconsistent. No repurposing system. No cross-posting automation. Stairway Legends newsletter, social accounts, and brand content all managed separately with no unified distribution engine.

04. Weekly Rhythm

AI Leverage Weekly — Strategy & Coaching

60-minute call split into two distinct modes. The first half captures what's bouncing around in Mike's and Caroline's heads. The second half is pure execution — decisions made, actions assigned.

Section 1: Brain Dump & Ideas (25 min)
0–5 min Quick wins from the week — what worked, what broke, what surprised
5–15 min Mike's content/tool sightings — anything he saw that sparked an idea. Gets captured to the backlog immediately.
15–25 min Caroline's pipeline updates — partnership leads, acquisition targets, investor conversations. Anything the AI should know about or act on.
Section 2: Direct Implementation Path (35 min)
25–35 min Andrew presents 1-2 recommendations with options already prepared. "Here's what I recommend, here's why, here's the alternative." Decision made on the call.
35–50 min Live building / demo of what was shipped that week. Screen share. Immediate feedback loop.
50–55 min Backlog review — reprioritize based on what we learned this week.
55–60 min Lock in next week's deliverables. Specific commitments from Andrew, from Mike, from Caroline. Written down. No ambiguity.

Key rule: Andrew shows up with recommendations already locked in. Call time is for decisions and feedback, not brainstorming from scratch.

06. The Operating Principle

Humans × AI × Code

Naval's 4 pillars of leverage applied to every decision. Nothing gets assigned to a human that AI can do. Nothing goes to AI that pure code can handle.

Humans Excel At

Strategic positioning decisions. Relationship building and sales calls. Brand voice and creative direction. Final approval on customer-facing assets. Partnership negotiations. Product feel and market intuition.

AI Excels At

Drafting copy and content at scale. Analyzing performance data and surfacing insights. Research and prospect scoring. Repurposing content across formats. Pattern recognition across thousands of data points.

Code Excels At

Data pipelines (Triple Whale to dashboard). Scheduled posting and distribution. CRM monitoring and alerts. Review scraping and sentiment tracking. API integrations between tools.
